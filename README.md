Motley — and it is absolutely unhinged in the best way.
The atmosphere is falling confetti — little rectangles in red, yellow, purple, and green tumbling slowly downward and sideways. The waveform is a four-color gradient sweeping all four jester colors across it. The cursor is a jester's marotte — a stick with a bell on top, a red dot and a purple dot on the shaft.

The case is a painted harpsichord with motley diamond panels in four colors along the lower half, a row of strings/jacks visible inside, hardware brackets, and a nameboard at the bottom that reads "Motley · Jester of the Road · Fear Nothing" in Cormorant italic. Above the keyboard: a jingle bell strip with 20 bells that animate and jingle on every keypress. Above that: a diamond-pattern case strip in motley colors.
The sound is proper harpsichord physics — zero attack ramp (a quill pluck is instant), sawtooth fundamental, harmonic series decaying with higher partials fading faster, and a fast key-release damp. No sustain pedal — when you lift your finger the string is silenced immediately, just like the real thing.
Registers — four stop combinations:

8′ — normal pitch, the main voice
4′ — an octave up, thinner and brighter
Lute — muted close sound, softer harmonics, intimate
Full — both 8′ and 4′ simultaneously, the big sound

Three temperaments — each one actually changes the frequencies:

Equal — standard modern tuning
Meantone — pure major thirds, that pre-Baroque warmth, wolf lurking
Pythagorean — pure fifths stacked, bright and slightly austere

The keys each have a color — red, yellow, purple, green cycling. Every key activates in its own color.
The Mood Meter tracks how fast you're playing and updates the header: sober → merry → rowdy → UNHINGED. A random jester quip appears in the lower right — "truth wears many colours," "what the sober man hides," "fools rush in — and land it."
Jingle toggle — 🔔 on: every note rings a random bell from the strip. 🔕 off: silence from the bells, you animal.
